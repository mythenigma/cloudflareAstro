# Design Portfolio Sharing: Create Links That Help You Get Hired

<div class="intro-panel">
  <p>Hiring managers spend less than 30 seconds on a first portfolio review. Your link needs to open fast, show your strongest work immediately, and not require a download, a login, or an app. Here's how to set that up.</p>
</div>

## Why a link beats an attachment for portfolios

- **Always current** — update your PDF and everyone who clicks the link sees the latest version, without you resending
- **No file size anxiety** — send a link to a 50 MB high-res portfolio without worrying about email limits
- **Track who opened it** — see which companies actually viewed your portfolio, and time follow-ups accordingly
- **Professional impression** — a clean link without "Dropbox" or "WeTransfer" in the URL reads as more polished

## Build role-specific portfolio links

Don't send one generic portfolio to every job. Create targeted versions:

- **Product design** — End-to-end UX process and outcomes. Include 3–4 case studies with before/after.
- **Brand / visual design** — Visual identity and campaigns. Include 5–6 varied brand projects.
- **UI / web design** — UI systems and interaction design. Include 4–5 screen-based projects.
- **Art direction** — Concept-to-execution breadth. Mixed format, strongest visual impact.

With MaiPDF you create separate links from separate PDFs — each with its own open tracking. You'll know which version got opened and when.

## Settings for hiring workflows

- **Download**: on or off — your call. Some designers want recruiters to have a copy for internal sharing; others prefer view-only
- **Expiry**: 30–60 days — aligned with typical job application cycles
- **Open limit**: 50–200 per link — leaves room for companies to share internally
- **Watermark**: optional — useful for client-facing concept work or unreleased projects

## First-page matters most

The first page is your cover. It should:
- State your name, role, and specialization in large text
- Link to your contact and online presence
- Set the tone visually — not be a table of contents

Recruiters who close a PDF on page 1 usually do so because the first page didn't communicate why they should keep reading.

## Track opens to time follow-ups

MaiPDF logs every open with a timestamp. Check [Control Center](https://www.maipdf.com/6/control-center.html) after you apply:

- **Opened same day** → strong interest, follow up within 24 hours
- **Opened multiple times** → probably being shown to multiple reviewers, team discussion underway
- **Not opened after 1 week** → application may not have reached the hiring manager, try outreach on another channel

## Keep links current

- Update your PDF directly from Control Center — the link URL doesn't change
- Retire old links from inactive application cycles
- Generate fresh links for new job search campaigns with an updated portfolio version
