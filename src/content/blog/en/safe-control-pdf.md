# Safe Control PDF: Manage Who Reads, Downloads, and Shares Your Documents

Sharing a PDF without controls is like mailing a letter without an envelope — anyone who sees it can copy, forward, or keep it forever. Safe PDF control means setting clear boundaries on what recipients can do with your document.

![Control Panel](/maipdf2026/how_to_control_panel.png)

## The Core Principle

Every shared document should answer three questions before it leaves your hands:

1. **Who** can access it?
2. **For how long** is access valid?
3. **What** can the viewer do with it (read only? download? print?)?

If you can't answer all three, your sharing method isn't safe enough.

## Five Essential Controls

### 1. View Limits
Set a maximum number of opens. After the limit is hit, the link returns an expiration notice. Use this for:
- Limited-edition content
- Exclusive proposals
- One-time-use documents

### 2. Expiration Dates
Auto-disable links after a specific date. The document becomes inaccessible — no manual intervention needed. Ideal for:
- Job offers with response deadlines
- Seasonal pricing sheets
- Time-limited legal agreements

### 3. Download Prevention
Allow reading in the browser but block the download button. The recipient can view every page but can't save a local copy. Combine with watermarking for maximum protection.

### 4. Dynamic Watermarks
Overlay the viewer's identity (email, IP, or custom text) on every page. This serves two purposes:
- **Deterrence** — viewers know they're identified, reducing screenshot/sharing risk
- **Traceability** — if the document leaks, you can identify the source

![Watermark Protection Flow](/maipdf2026/flowchart/en-watermark-protection-flow.svg)

### 5. Link Revocation
Disable a link instantly from the control panel. The document is no longer accessible to anyone — regardless of how many people have the URL.

## Access Decision Logic

Not sure which controls to apply? Follow this decision flow:

![Access Control Decision](/maipdf2026/flowchart/en-access-control-decision.svg)

**Public content** → no limits, downloads enabled
**Internal review** → view limit, no downloads, expiration
**Confidential** → all controls on, plus email verification and watermark

## Monitoring After Sharing

Controls aren't just set-and-forget. MaiPDF provides real-time access records:

- Who opened the document
- When and how many times
- From what device and location
- Whether they attempted blocked actions (download, print)

![Access Records](/maipdf2026/page_redirect_to_accessRecord.png)

Review these records periodically, especially for high-value documents. If you see unexpected access patterns, tighten controls or revoke the link.

## Common Mistakes

- **Setting view limits too low** — page refreshes count as views; give at least 3–5× your expected audience size
- **Forgetting expiration on temporary content** — old proposals and outdated pricing should not be accessible indefinitely
- **Using download prevention alone** — screenshots bypass download blocks; add watermarks for meaningful protection
