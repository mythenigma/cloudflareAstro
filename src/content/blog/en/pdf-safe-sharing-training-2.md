# PDF Safe Sharing Training: Teach Your Team to Share Documents Securely

Most document security breaches aren't caused by hackers — they're caused by employees who don't know the difference between a public link and a controlled one. This training guide gives your team the knowledge to share PDFs safely, every time.

![User Control Panel](/maipdf2026/user_control_panel_alotof_functions.png)

## Module 1: Why Sharing Method Matters

The same PDF shared two different ways creates completely different security profiles:

| Method | Who Can Access | Can They Forward? | Can You Revoke? | Analytics? |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Email attachment | Anyone who receives the email chain | Yes, unlimitedly | No | None |
| Cloud drive link (public) | Anyone with the URL | Yes | Yes (manually) | Basic |
| MaiPDF controlled link | Only within configured limits | Link dies at view limit | Yes (instant) | Full |

**Training takeaway**: always use controlled links for anything that isn't explicitly public.

## Module 2: The Five Controls Everyone Should Know

### View Limits
Set how many times the document can be opened total. Once exhausted, the link stops working.

### Expiration Dates
The document auto-disables after a specific date. No manual action needed.

### Download Prevention
Recipients can read in the browser but can't save a copy to their device.

### Watermarks
The viewer's identity (email, IP, or custom text) appears on every page — visible deterrent and forensic tool.

### Email Verification
Require the viewer to enter and verify their email before accessing the document. Creates an access log tied to real identities.

## Module 3: The Standard Sharing Workflow

Train your team on this repeatable process:

**Step 1: Classify** — Is the document public, internal, confidential, or restricted?

**Step 2: Upload** — Go to [maipdf.com](https://www.maipdf.com) and upload the PDF.

**Step 3: Configure** — Apply controls based on classification:

| Classification | View Limit | Expiration | Download | Watermark |
|---------------|-----------|------------|----------|-----------|
| Public | None | Optional | On | Off |
| Internal | 100+ | 90 days | Off | Off |
| Confidential | 20–50 | 30 days | Off | On |
| Restricted | 10–20 | 7 days | Off | On + email verification |

**Step 4: Share** — Copy the link and send through appropriate channels.

**Step 5: Monitor** — Check access records within 24 hours.

## Module 4: Common Mistakes to Recognize

### Mistake: "I'll just attach it to the email"
**Problem**: no controls, no tracking, no revocation
**Fix**: upload first, share the link

### Mistake: "Everyone in the company can see this"
**Problem**: "everyone" includes people who shouldn't, and the document lives forever
**Fix**: use view limits and expiration even for internal documents

### Mistake: "It's just a draft, security doesn't matter"
**Problem**: drafts often contain more sensitive information than final versions (comments, tracked changes, internal pricing)
**Fix**: drafts need *more* security, not less

### Mistake: "I set it up once, I don't need to check"
**Problem**: unusual access patterns go unnoticed
**Fix**: review access records periodically for important documents

## Module 5: Emergency Response

If a team member realizes a document was shared inappropriately:

1. **Immediately** revoke the link in MaiPDF
2. **Check** access records to see who already viewed it
3. **Report** to the appropriate security/compliance contact
4. **Create** a new link with tighter controls if the document still needs to be shared

**The key advantage of link-based sharing**: you can shut it down instantly. With attachments, there's nothing you can do.

## Training Assessment

After this training, every team member should be able to:

1. Explain why links are safer than attachments
2. Choose appropriate security controls for different document types
3. Follow the standard sharing workflow
4. Recognize common mistakes
5. Execute the emergency response procedure
