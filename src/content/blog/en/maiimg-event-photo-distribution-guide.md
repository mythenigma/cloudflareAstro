# MaiImg Event Photo Distribution Guide

Event photography creates unique distribution challenges: hundreds of photos need to reach specific attendees quickly, but you don't want uncontrolled images floating across the internet. MaiImg solves this with secure, trackable image sharing.

![MaiImg Platform](/maiimg/maiimg-head.png)

## The Event Photo Distribution Problem

After every conference, wedding, corporate event, or trade show, photographers face the same headaches:

- **Volume** — dozens to hundreds of images need distribution
- **Audience** — specific people should receive specific photos
- **Speed** — attendees want photos immediately, not weeks later
- **Control** — event organizers want to approve which images go public
- **Tracking** — knowing which photos were accessed helps plan future events

## How MaiImg Handles Event Photos

### Step 1: Upload
Drag and drop your event photos (PNG, JPG, WebP — up to 25 files at once, 50 MB each) to [MaiImg](https://www.maiimg.com).

### Step 2: Generate Links and QR Codes
Each upload generates a unique shareable link and QR code. For events, QR codes are especially powerful:

- Print QR codes on event programs
- Display QR codes on screens during the event
- Include QR codes in follow-up emails

![Image Result](/maiimg/image_result.png)

### Step 3: Set Access Controls
Configure per-image controls:

| Control | Event Use Case |
|---------|---------------|
| View limit | Limit access to registered attendees |
| Expiration | Auto-disable after the event closes |
| Download toggle | Allow or prevent photo downloads |

![MaiImg Settings](/maiimg/maiimg-setting.png)

### Step 4: Distribute
Share QR codes at the venue, send links via email, or post to event-specific channels. Each method tracks independently.

### Step 5: Monitor
Check which photos were viewed most, which attendees engaged, and when peak access occurred.

![Tracking](/maiimg/result_tracking.png)

## Distribution Strategies by Event Type

### Corporate Conference
- Upload keynote slides and session photos
- QR codes on lanyards or session schedules
- Disable downloads for sensitive presentation content
- Enable downloads for networking photos

### Wedding
- Create separate galleries: ceremony, reception, portraits
- Share QR codes at the reception for real-time viewing
- Set high view limits (guests will share with family)
- Enable downloads for personal photos

### Trade Show
- Product demo photos with brand watermarks
- QR codes on booth signage
- Track which products generate the most photo engagement
- Disable downloads for competitive intelligence protection

### Charity / Fundraiser
- Event highlight photos shared with donors
- QR codes on thank-you materials
- Track engagement for donor reporting

## Real-Time Distribution During Events

The fastest workflow:
1. Photographer captures shots
2. Quick upload to MaiImg between sessions
3. QR code displayed on event screens or social media
4. Attendees scan and view immediately

This creates a live photo feed without requiring any app installation from attendees.

## Post-Event Analytics

After the event, review MaiImg's access records:

- **Most viewed photos** — highlight these in post-event communications
- **Peak access times** — understand when attendees were most engaged
- **Device breakdown** — optimize future photo sizes for the dominant device type
- **Geographic spread** — see if images reached beyond the event location

## Getting Started

Upload your event photos at [maiimg.com](https://www.maiimg.com), generate QR codes, and distribute instantly. Every view is tracked, every image is controlled.
