# PDF Access Control: Viewing Limits for Sensitive Document Distribution

When you distribute a sensitive document, you're making a trust decision. Viewing limits minimize the risk surface of that decision by declaring upfront: this document can be accessed X times, and then it's gone.

![Control Panel](/maipdf2026/how_to_control_panel.png)

## Why Sensitive Documents Need Viewing Limits

Sensitive documents have a common problem: they're needed by specific people for a specific purpose, but once shared, they tend to circulate beyond the intended audience.

- A **salary benchmarking report** sent to a manager gets forwarded to the entire team
- A **draft contract** shared with one lawyer ends up in a competitor's hands
- An **M&A briefing** intended for the board reaches the press

Viewing limits don't make leaks impossible, but they dramatically reduce exposure. A document that can only be opened 20 times is far less dangerous than one with unlimited access.

## Industry Applications

### Legal
**What they share**: contracts, NDAs, litigation documents, discovery materials
**Typical limit**: 10–30 views
**Why**: only parties to the agreement should see the document; low limits ensure it doesn't circulate in legal networks

### Human Resources
**What they share**: compensation data, performance reviews, disciplinary records
**Typical limit**: 5–15 views
**Why**: HR documents contain personal information; viewing limits support data minimization principles

### Finance
**What they share**: quarterly forecasts, audit reports, investment memos
**Typical limit**: 20–50 views
**Why**: material non-public information must be tightly controlled; viewing limits create a natural access cap

### Education
**What they share**: exam papers, answer keys, grade sheets
**Typical limit**: varies widely
**Why**: exam integrity requires that answer keys have minimal exposure; student rosters have privacy constraints

## Calculating the Right Limit

**Formula**: `target audience × expected reads per person × buffer`

Example: sharing a report with 5 executives who each need to read it 2–3 times:
- 5 × 3 = 15
- Add 50% buffer: 15 × 1.5 = ~23
- Set limit: 25

The buffer accounts for page refreshes, device switches, and re-reads.

## Combining Viewing Limits with Watermarks

This combination is particularly powerful for sensitive documents:

1. **Viewing limit** restricts the total number of accesses
2. **Watermark** identifies each viewer, making them accountable

If the document leaks despite the viewing limit, the watermark traces it to a specific person. If there's no leak, the viewing limit ensures the document wasn't accessed more than expected.

## Monitoring and Response

After sharing a sensitive document with viewing limits:

**Check access records within 24 hours** — verify that the expected recipients are the ones accessing it.

**Watch for rapid view count depletion** — if the limit is consumed faster than expected, the link may have been forwarded.

**Revoke if suspicious** — disable the link immediately if access patterns suggest unauthorized sharing.
