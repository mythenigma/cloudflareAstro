# PDF QR Code Generation Tutorial: Create Scannable Links for Any Document

A QR code turns any PDF into a scannable, instantly-accessible document. No typing URLs, no searching through emails — point your phone camera at the code and start reading.

![QR Code Result](/maipdf2026/Result_of_qr_link.png)

## Why QR Codes for PDFs?

QR codes bridge the gap between physical and digital:

| Use Case | Physical Placement | Digital Content |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Business card | Printed QR on cardstock | Links to your portfolio PDF |
| Product packaging | QR on the box | Links to instruction manual |
| Conference poster | QR on printed poster | Links to full research paper |
| Restaurant menu | QR on table tent | Links to full-page menu PDF |
| Real estate listing | QR on yard sign | Links to property brochure |

## Step-by-Step Tutorial

### Step 1: Upload Your PDF
Go to [maipdf.com](https://www.maipdf.com) and upload your document. Drag-and-drop or click to browse — files up to 100 MB are supported.

### Step 2: Get the QR Code
After upload, MaiPDF automatically generates both a shareable link and a QR code. The QR code is displayed alongside the link on the results page.

![Generated QR and Link](/maipdf2026/Result_of_qr_link.png)

### Step 3: Download the QR Code
Right-click the QR code image and save it, or copy it to your clipboard. The QR code is generated at high resolution, suitable for both digital displays and print materials.

### Step 4: Place the QR Code
Add the QR code to your materials:
- **Print**: business cards, flyers, posters, packaging, signage
- **Digital**: presentations, emails, social media posts, websites
- **Video**: display during webinars, tutorials, or livestreams

### Step 5: Test Before Publishing
Always scan the QR code with your own phone before publishing. Verify that:
- The PDF loads correctly
- The viewing experience is smooth on mobile
- Any access controls (password, email verification) work as expected

## QR Code + Access Controls

The QR code points to the same managed link as the URL. All controls apply:

| Control | Works with QR? |
|---------|---------------|
| View limit | Yes — each scan counts as a view |
| Expiration | Yes — QR stops working after the date |
| Download prevention | Yes — scanned viewers can read but not save |
| Watermark | Yes — scanner's identity appears on every page |
| Email verification | Yes — scanner must verify email before viewing |

## Tracking QR Code Scans

Every QR code scan generates an access record in MaiPDF:

- **Scan count** — total number of times the QR was scanned
- **Device type** — overwhelmingly mobile for QR codes
- **Time and location** — when and where people scanned
- **Engagement** — how long they viewed the document

Use this data to measure:
- Which physical placements drive the most scans
- Peak scan times (for events and marketing campaigns)
- Geographic distribution of your audience

## Design Tips for QR Codes

**Size matters**: QR codes should be at least 2cm × 2cm (0.8" × 0.8") in print. For scanning from a distance (posters, banners), go larger.

**Contrast**: the QR code needs high contrast against its background. Black on white is the safest choice.

**Quiet zone**: leave white space around the QR code. Crowding it with other design elements reduces scan reliability.

**Add a call to action**: don't just place a QR code — tell people what they'll get. "Scan for the full brochure" converts better than a bare QR code.

## Updating the Document Behind the QR

A major advantage of MaiPDF QR codes: you can replace the PDF without regenerating the QR code. The QR points to a link; the link points to the document. Swap the document, and every future scan shows the new content.

This is perfect for:
- **Menus** that change seasonally
- **Catalogs** that update with new products
- **Manuals** that get revised over time
