# PDF Online Preview: Let Recipients Read Without Downloading

When you share a PDF as a download link, you're asking the recipient to: click, wait, find the file, open it in a reader, and then start reading. That's five steps before they see a single word. An online preview reduces that to one: click and read.

![PDF Native View](/maipdf2026/pdf native view on ui.png)

## Why Online Preview Matters

**Faster engagement** — the document loads instantly in the browser tab. No file manager, no dedicated app.

**Works everywhere** — modern browsers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) all render PDFs natively. Your recipients don't need Acrobat or any other software.

**Mobile-first** — phone users especially benefit. Downloading and finding a PDF on a phone is painful. A preview link opens inline and scrolls naturally.

**Reduced friction** — every extra step between "received the link" and "reading the content" loses a percentage of your audience. Preview eliminates those steps.

## Preview vs. Download: When to Use Which

| Scenario | Best Approach | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| Sales proposal | Preview only | Prevent unauthorized forwarding |
| Printable worksheet | Preview + download | Reader may need a physical copy |
| Confidential report | Preview only + watermark | Track who views, discourage screenshots |
| Public brochure | Preview + download | Maximize accessibility |

You can toggle download permissions per document on MaiPDF. The default is preview-only, which gives you maximum control.

## Protecting Content During Preview

Online preview doesn't mean unprotected content. MaiPDF adds several layers:

- **No right-click save** — the standard "Save As" option is disabled in the viewer
- **Dynamic watermark** — overlay the viewer's email or IP on every page to discourage screenshots
- **View limits** — set the maximum number of times the document can be opened
- **Expiration** — auto-disable the link after a specific date
