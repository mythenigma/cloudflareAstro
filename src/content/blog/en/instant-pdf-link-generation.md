**Instant** here means: after you upload, you get a **URL** and usually a **QR code** you can drop into email, Slack, or a calendar invite—once you click **Create Secure Link** on the [main MaiPDF tool](https://maipdf.com/pdf/maipdf2026.html).

The speed is real. What keeps it professional is **Configure**—the step worth an extra half minute.

## The three steps (same as the live product)

1. **Upload file** — drag or pick your PDF.  
2. **Configure** — set how long and how often the link may be used, plus optional alerts and checks.  
3. **Share** — copy URL / show QR; save your **Read** and **Modify** codes for later.

![Upload: get the file into step 1](/maipdf2026/maipdf_header_login_or_upload_file.png)

## What “Configure” lets you set

On [maipdf.com/pdf/maipdf2026.html](https://maipdf.com/pdf/maipdf2026.html) the middle step includes:

| Area | Plain meaning |
|------|----------------|
| **Access limit** | How many times the PDF may be opened. |
| **Each session** | How long one continuous read may last. |
| **Expiration** | Choices such as 1 hour, 24 hours, 5 days, custom days, or unlimited—shown in **UTC** and **local** time. |
| **Read alerts (Telegram)** | Optional “someone opened it” pings; add [@maipdfbot](https://t.me/maipdfbot) and your `chat_id`. |
| **Email verification** | Reader verifies email before opening. |
| **SecureView / FenceView / Unrestricted** | Tighter protected viewing, stronger deterrence, or lighter limits—see the on-page help text. |
| **Dynamic watermark** | When on, can show reader/time cues on the document. |

> **Note:** If **access limit** is set **above 10,000** opens, MaiPDF **does not** log per-open records, **disables** Telegram alerts, and **disables** dynamic watermark. For normal sharing, use ordinary limits so those features stay available.

## After you create the link

You typically get:

- **Copy this link**  
- **QR** for phones or slides  
- **Read code** (for opening) and **Modify code** (for managing the link)  
- Actions such as **Change file** or **Access records** when needed  

![Result screen: link, QR, codes, and shortcuts](/maipdf2026/Result_of_qr_link.png)

## Before you blast the URL: quick checks

1. **Expiration** matches how sensitive the file is.  
2. **Access limit** matches how many real readers you expect.  
3. **Email verification** on when you need known recipients.  
4. **Telegram alerts** on when you want pings (and limits allow).  
5. Open the link **once** in a private window on desktop and phone.

## Common mistakes

- Sharing **before** reviewing Configure.  
- Reusing **one** private link in **many** public places.  
- Treating “instant” as “no expiry needed” for external sends.

## Summary

- **Upload → Configure → Share** matches what you see on maipdf.com.  
- **Expiry, limits, Telegram, email verification** turn raw speed into something you can defend.  
- **Self-test** every important link.
