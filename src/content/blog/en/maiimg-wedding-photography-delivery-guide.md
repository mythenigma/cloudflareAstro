## Tips for Professional Wedding Photo Delivery

### 1. Create Multiple Galleries

Don't overwhelm couples with one massive gallery. Break it down:
- By time of day
- By event (ceremony, reception)
- By people (bridal party, family)
- By style (candid, posed, details)

### 2. Use QR Codes Strategically

- Print on wedding favors
- Include in thank-you cards
- Display at reception
- Share via wedding website

### 3. Set Appropriate Expiration Dates

- Engagement photos: 30 days
- Wedding day sneak peeks: 7 days
- Full gallery: 90 days
- Vendor galleries: 30 days

### 4. Track Engagement

Monitor which galleries get most views to understand:
- Client preferences
- Popular photo styles
- Best delivery timing
- Family engagement levels

## FAQ: Wedding Photography Delivery

### How many photos can I share per gallery?

You can upload up to 25 images per gallery. For larger weddings, create multiple galleries organized by category or time of day.

### Can I update galleries after sharing?

Yes! You can delete and recreate galleries, or create new ones with updated selections. The link changes, so you'll need to share the new link.

### How do I share photos during the wedding?

Generate a QR code for a "Sneak Peeks" gallery and display it at the reception. Guests can scan and view photos in real-time.

### Can couples download photos?

Yes, you can enable downloads in the gallery settings. This allows couples to save their favorite photos.

### How long do galleries stay active?

You control the expiration date. Set it from 1 day to 1 year, or leave it unlimited. Typical wedding galleries are set to 90 days.
