# Freelancer Portfolio Links: Win More Clients with Clear Sharing

<div class="intro-panel">
  <p>Clients judge your work before they read a word. If your portfolio is a 20 MB email attachment that takes ages to load, you've already lost ground. A controlled link opens instantly in the browser, looks professional, and gives you data on who actually viewed it.</p>

![Clean PDF portfolio view in MaiPDF](/maipdf2026/show_off/goodlookingshow.png)

## The problem with how most freelancers share work

- **Email attachments** — bounce in inboxes, hit size limits, end up in spam
- **Google Drive / Dropbox** — forces recipients to log in or see a cluttered file-manager UI
- **Your own website** — public to everyone, no control, no analytics per prospect
- **WeTransfer-style links** — expire automatically, no view tracking, looks disposable

What you actually need: a link that opens instantly on any device, shows your work in a clean viewer, tells you when someone looked, and lets you turn it off when the deal closes.

## How a link-based portfolio workflow works

1. **Build your portfolio PDF** — keep it under 10 pages. Lead with your 3 strongest projects.
2. **Upload to MaiPDF** — drag and drop, no registration needed.
3. **Set your rules** — disable download (protect your work), set an open limit or expiry.
4. **Send the link** — paste it in your proposal email, DM, or cold outreach.
5. **Check who opened** — view access records before your follow-up call.

![Upload and get a shareable link with one click](/maipdf2026/show_off/shareandsend.png)

## Tailor links to each prospect

Don't send every client the same portfolio. Create separate links for different audiences:

| Prospect type | Portfolio focus | Suggested settings |
|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Brand identity client** | Logo, brand guide, packaging | Download off, 30-day expiry |
| **Web design lead** | Landing pages, UI screenshots | Watermark on, open limit 20 |
| **Print/editorial** | Magazine layouts, book covers | Download off, no expiry |
| **Quick pitch** | 3 best projects only | 7-day expiry, open limit 10 |

Each link has its own access records. You'll know which version got opened and how often — useful for adjusting your approach.

## What the client sees

Your PDF opens directly in a clean viewer — no login, no download prompts, no file-manager chrome. It looks like your work, not like a cloud storage service.

![Viewer interface — clean, distraction-free](/maipdf2026/show_off/showpdf.png)

On mobile, the viewer scales automatically. The client can flip through pages wherever they are — on the commute, in a meeting, on their phone.

## Protect your work

Freelancers lose work to screenshot-and-steal all the time. A link with the right settings reduces that risk:

- **Download disabled** — no save button in the viewer
- **Print disabled** — no print option
- **Watermark** — your name or email burned into every page
- **Open limit** — link stops working after N opens, so it can't circulate forever

![No-print, no-download controls in action](/maipdf2026/show_off/viewercontainer_noprint_nodownlaod.png)

None of these are bulletproof against a determined screen-recorder, but they stop casual theft and make it clear you treat your work seriously.

## Use access data to time your follow-up

MaiPDF logs every open with a timestamp. Use that signal:

- **Opened within 24h** → they're interested. Follow up while it's fresh.
- **Opened 3+ times** → they're comparing you to others. Send a note highlighting what makes your approach different.
- **Never opened** → your email may have been missed. Resend with a different subject line.

![Access records show who opened and when](/maipdf2026/page_redirect_to_accessRecord.png)

This is free data that most freelancers ignore because they share via attachment (no tracking) or cloud links (analytics buried in dashboards they never check).
