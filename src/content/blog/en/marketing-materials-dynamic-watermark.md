# Marketing Materials Dynamic Watermark: Protect Branded Content

Marketing teams create high-value content — brochures, partner price lists, competitive comparison charts, unreleased product previews — that needs to be shared but shouldn't be leaked. Dynamic watermarks protect this content without slowing down distribution.

![Watermark Example](/maipdf2026/dynamic_water_mark_example.jpg)

## The Marketing Content Dilemma

Marketing teams face a unique tension:

- **Need to share widely** — partners, distributors, clients, and agencies all need access to materials
- **Need to control distribution** — pricing, unreleased products, and competitive data shouldn't reach competitors
- **Need to maintain brand quality** — ugly security measures (garbled links, complex passwords) undermine the professional image

Dynamic watermarks resolve this tension: the document is easy to access and read, but every viewer sees their own identity on every page.

## Which Marketing Materials Benefit Most

| Material | Watermark Value | Why |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| Partner price lists | High | Prevents competitors from obtaining your pricing through partners |
| Unreleased product previews | High | Traces leaks back to specific preview recipients |
| Competitive analysis decks | High | Contains internal strategy that shouldn't reach competitors |
| Event brochures | Medium | Protects pre-event announcements |
| Case studies | Low | Usually intended for broad distribution |
| Public press kits | None | Designed to be shared freely |

## Real Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Product Preview
You send a product preview to 20 key partners before the public launch. Each gets a uniquely watermarked link. Two weeks later, a screenshot appears on a competitor's blog — the watermark identifies Partner X as the source.

### Scenario 2: Regional Pricing
Different regions receive different pricing sheets. Dynamic watermarks ensure that a distributor in Region A can't share Region B's lower pricing with their customers.

### Scenario 3: Agency Brief
You share a creative brief with your advertising agency. The watermark ensures the brief doesn't circulate to the agency's other clients (who might be your competitors).
