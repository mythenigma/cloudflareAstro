# Instant PDF Sharing with QR Codes

<div class="intro-panel">
  <p>The fastest way to share a PDF with a room of people is a QR code. No email list to manage, no USB drive, no login required. Display the code, they scan it, they're reading. Here's how to set it up properly.</p>
</div>

## How it works (the full flow)

1. Upload your PDF at [maipdf.com](https://maipdf.com)
2. Configure your settings (open limit, expiry, download on/off)
3. Click **Create Secure Link** — you get a URL and a QR code
4. Download or screenshot the QR code
5. Display it in your presentation, on print, or in a digital asset
6. Audience scans with their phone camera and reads immediately in a browser

![Link and QR code generated together — use either or both](/maipdf2026/Result_of_qr_link.png)

No app required for the scanner. Any modern phone camera or QR scanner app opens the link directly.

## Where to display the QR code

| Context | How to display | Tips |
|---------|----------------|------|
| Presentation slide | Add as image on last slide | Make it at least 200x200px |
| Printed handout | Print on flyer, poster, or card | Include short URL below QR as fallback |
| Event badge or table card | Print directly | Test scan distance before printing |
| Email footer | Embed as image | Useful for mobile-first recipients |
| In-store display | On signage or product | High contrast background for scanning |

## Set access controls before you display

Once the QR is shown publicly, you can't control who scans it — only who the link allows in:

- **Open limit** — set to expected audience + buffer (e.g., event has 200 people, set 500)
- **Expiry** — set to end-of-event or end-of-week so the link doesn't stay live forever
- **Download** — on or off depending on whether you want attendees to save a copy
- **Email verification** — optional but adds accountability if the audience is specific

![Configure all controls before generating the QR code](/maipdf2026/MaiPDF_settings_expiration_telegram.png)

## Good use cases for QR PDF distribution

**Conference or workshop:** Display QR on opening slide. Attendees scan during break. Slides, notes, or supplementary materials load immediately — no need to collect email addresses or send follow-up emails.

**Product brochure or catalog:** Print QR on physical packaging or display. Customer scans and sees current product PDF. You can update the PDF without reprinting.

**Classroom materials:** Students scan at the start of class. Teacher controls open limit so only enrolled students in the room access it.

**Restaurant or event menu:** Print QR on table cards. Menu loads as PDF — update when prices change, no reprint needed.

## After the QR is distributed

The QR code links to the same URL as your shareable link. From [Control Center](https://www.maipdf.com/6/control-center.html) you can:
- See how many times it was scanned (access records)
- Swap the file if the PDF needs updating (QR stays valid)
- Kill the link when the event is over
