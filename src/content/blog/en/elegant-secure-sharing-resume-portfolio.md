# Elegant, Secure Sharing for Resumes and Portfolios

Sending your resume as an email attachment feels outdated — it lands in a cluttered inbox alongside hundreds of other PDFs named `Resume_Final_v3.pdf`. A secure, trackable link makes a better impression and gives you visibility into whether your application was actually opened.

## Why Links Beat Attachments for Job Applications

| Email Attachment | Secure PDF Link |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Gets buried in inbox | Clean URL in your email, LinkedIn, or website |
| No way to know if opened | View tracking shows exactly when it was read |
| Can't update after sending | Replace the PDF; same link shows updated version |
| Anyone can forward it indefinitely | Set view limits or revoke access |
| Downloaded and forgotten | Opens instantly in the browser |

## How It Works

1. **Upload** your resume or portfolio PDF to [maipdf.com](https://www.maipdf.com)
2. **Configure** sharing settings (download on/off, view tracking)
3. **Share** the link wherever you need it

That's it. The recruiter clicks the link, your resume renders instantly in the browser — no download needed, no compatibility issues.

## Recommended Settings by Situation

### Active Job Search
- **Download**: ON — recruiters often need to save and share internally
- **View limit**: 50+ — your link may be shared among hiring teams
- **Expiration**: none while searching
- **Why**: you want maximum visibility; make it easy for recruiters

### Selective Application (Dream Job)
- **Download**: ON
- **View limit**: 20
- **Expiration**: 30 days after application deadline
- **Why**: enough views for the hiring committee; auto-expires if you don't get the role

### Freelance Portfolio
- **Download**: OFF — protect your work samples
- **View limit**: unlimited
- **Expiration**: none (evergreen portfolio)
- **Why**: clients should see your work but not download and use it without hiring you

### Confidential Job Search
- **Download**: OFF
- **View limit**: 10 per company
- **Expiration**: 2 weeks
- **Why**: if you're discreetly job-searching while employed, limit distribution and set short expiration

## View Tracking: Your Secret Advantage

Most job applicants never know whether their resume was opened. With view tracking, you know:

- **Was it opened at all?** — if 5 days pass with zero views, follow up
- **When was it opened?** — multiple views in a short window often means the hiring team is discussing you
- **How many times?** — many views = strong interest; consider preparing for an interview

This turns the black hole of job applications into actionable data.

## Portfolio-Specific Tips

For designers, photographers, architects, and other visual professionals:

### Optimize Your PDF for Online Viewing
- **File size**: keep under 20 MB for fast loading
- **Resolution**: 150 DPI is enough for screen viewing; save file size for web
- **Layout**: design for horizontal scrolling if your portfolio is landscape-oriented
- **First page**: make it count — it's the only page a busy recruiter might see

### Multiple Portfolio Versions
Create different PDFs for different audiences:
- **General portfolio** — your best 10-15 pieces
- **Industry-specific** — curated for the specific role (app design, print design, etc.)
- **Case study format** — deep dives on 3-5 projects for senior roles

Upload each as a separate link. Send the most relevant version to each contact.
