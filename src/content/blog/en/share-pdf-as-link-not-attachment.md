# Share PDFs as Links, Not Attachments

<div class="intro-panel">
  <p>Sending a PDF as an attachment is like printing 50 copies and mailing them out. Once sent, you can't update them, can't see who read them, and can't stop them from being forwarded. A link gives you all of that back.</p>
</div>

![Share your PDF link by email, chat, or QR code worldwide](/maipdf2026/show_off/share pdf wordwide.png)

## What changes when you share by link

**Version control.** Update the document and everybody who clicks the link sees the latest version — not the one they downloaded into their inbox 3 weeks ago.

**Revocability.** Made a mistake? Shared too broadly? Go to [Control Center](https://www.maipdf.com/6/control-center.html) and kill the link in one click. An attachment you already sent is gone forever.

**Visibility.** MaiPDF logs every open with a timestamp. You'll know if the client read the proposal before you follow up — instead of guessing.

**No size limits.** Email attachments cap out around 25 MB. Links have no such restriction.

**Mobile experience.** Recipients open the PDF in a browser viewer — no "please open in Adobe Reader" prompts, no app downloads, no portrait-mode formatting disasters.

## How to share a PDF as a link

1. Upload your PDF at [maipdf.com](https://maipdf.com) — no signup required.
2. Choose your settings (download on/off, expiry, view limit).
3. Click **Create Secure Link**.
4. Paste the URL in your email or chat — or use the QR code for in-person contexts.

## When attachments still make sense

- The recipient's environment actively blocks external URLs (some government or enterprise IT policies)
- Legal workflow requires an immutable local file exchange
- The recipient explicitly asked for an attachment for their records

For everything else, a link is the better default.
